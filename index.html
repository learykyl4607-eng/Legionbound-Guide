Core Mechanic

How Class Upgrades Work

Legionbound uses a proximity-based upgrade system. Classes are arranged in two synergy loops — place a class next to the one that follows it in the loop and it will teach that unit how to upgrade.

The Rule

Each class teaches the next class in its loop how to upgrade. Position the teacher adjacent to the student on the battlefield to trigger the upgrade. The arrows in the infographic show the teaching direction.

For example: a Warrior standing next to a Lumberjack teaches the Lumberjack to upgrade. A Shieldmage standing next to a Guardian teaches the Guardian to upgrade.

There are three ways to get classes into your roster in the first place: you start with five, unlock others through achievements, and unlock the rest via the skill tree.

Available From The Start

These five classes are available immediately with no unlocking required. Notice that they give you entry points into both loops right away — Warrior and Cleric on the Heavy side, Ranger, Rogue and Wizard spread across key positions.

Starting Strategy

Warrior and Cleric give you two separate entry points into the Heavy loop immediately. Position them near their respective students early — Warrior near a Lumberjack slot, Cleric near a Witch slot — to start teaching chains as soon as possible.

What This Means For Skill Tree Planning

Since Lumberjack, Priest, Witch, Herbalist, Monk, Barkeep, Artificer, and Duelist all come free from achievements, don't spend skill tree points on classes you'll unlock anyway. Focus skill tree points on classes that aren't available through achievements — especially ones that sit at the start of long teaching chains.

Third Unlock Method

Skill Tree — Optimal Unlock Order

Fourteen classes must be unlocked through the skill tree. The order you unlock them significantly affects how quickly you can build upgrade chains. Prioritize classes that sit at the head of the longest teaching sequences, and avoid spending points on classes that are covered by achievements.

Easy Tier — 6 classes
Mercenary · Guardian · Swashbuckler · Brawler · Druid · Shieldmage
1
Druid
Druid → Wizard (starter) → Battlemage → Shieldmage
Your starter Wizard is already on the field teaching Battlemage. Unlocking Druid adds a teacher for Wizard itself, extending the chain backwards and giving you three connected upgrades from a single unlock.
2
Guardian
Shieldmage → Guardian → Smith → Knight (→ Priest, earned via achievement)
Unlocking Guardian opens Smith and Knight downstream. Priest comes free from achievements, so once Knight is teaching Priest you get the full Smith → Knight → Priest chain without extra spend.
3
Swashbuckler
Swashbuckler → Gunslinger → Duelist (achievement) → Rogue (starter)
Swashbuckler kicks off the longest unlock chain in the Agile loop. Duelist comes free from achievements and Rogue is a starter, so you only need to unlock Swashbuckler and Gunslinger to complete this entire segment.
4
Brawler
Mercenary → Brawler → Barbarian → Warrior (starter)
Brawler teaches Barbarian which wraps the Heavy loop back to your starter Warrior. Mercenary is also in Easy tier so both will be available — unlock Brawler first since it's the longer chain anchor.
5
Mercenary
Tax Collector (achievement) → Mercenary → Brawler (already unlocked)
Tax Collector comes free from achievements, so unlocking Mercenary completes that segment automatically. With Brawler already on the field this closes a tight three-class chain with only one skill point.
6
Shieldmage
Battlemage → Shieldmage → Guardian (already unlocked)
Last in Easy tier because Guardian is already unlocked and Battlemage will be available through Wizard's teaching chain. Unlock Shieldmage to close the Battlemage → Shieldmage → Guardian segment.
Medium Tier — 8 classes
Gunslinger · Paladin · Samurai · Knight · Bard · Sorcerer · Barbarian · Battlemage · Smith
1
Battlemage
Wizard (starter) → Battlemage → Shieldmage (Easy unlock)
Your starter Wizard is already teaching towards Battlemage. Unlocking it here connects the Druid → Wizard chain from Easy tier all the way through to Shieldmage and Guardian.
2
Smith
Guardian (Easy unlock) → Smith → Knight → Priest (achievement)
With Guardian already unlocked, Smith slots directly into the chain. Knight and Priest follow immediately — Priest is free from achievements so this three-step chain costs only one skill point.
3
Sorcerer
Witch (achievement) → Sorcerer → Tax Collector (achievement)
Both Witch and Tax Collector come free from achievements. Unlocking Sorcerer fills the only gap in this three-class segment of the Heavy loop for a single skill point.
4
Paladin
Priest (achievement) → Paladin → Cleric (starter)
Priest is free from achievements and Cleric is a starter — unlocking Paladin is the single link that completes this entire three-class segment with no further spend.
5
Barbarian
Brawler (Easy unlock) → Barbarian → Warrior (starter)
Brawler is already unlocked and Warrior is a starter. Unlocking Barbarian completes the loop wrap on the Heavy side — a single point closes the full Heavy loop.
6
Gunslinger
Swashbuckler (Easy unlock) → Gunslinger → Duelist (achievement)
Swashbuckler is already on the field and Duelist comes from achievements. One point fills the gap and completes the Swashbuckler → Gunslinger → Duelist → Rogue chain in the Agile loop.
7
Knight
Smith (already unlocked) → Knight → Priest (achievement)
Smith is already unlocked above and Priest is free. Knight fills the last gap in the Smith → Knight → Priest chain on the Heavy loop.
8
Bard
Monk (achievement) → Bard → Swashbuckler (Easy unlock)
Monk comes free and Swashbuckler is already unlocked. Bard is the single missing link in the Monk → Bard → Swashbuckler chain on the Agile loop.
9
Samurai
Rogue (starter) → Samurai → Artificer (achievement)
Unlock last — Rogue is a starter and Artificer comes free from achievements. Samurai completes the Agile loop wrap, giving you the full circular chain on both loops.
